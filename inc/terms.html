Hate speech

Content which constitutes hate speech will not be accepted.

Hate speech comprises any expression which tends to harass, intimidate, or offend a person or group of people based on an immutable characteristic that the group shares.

What is an immutable characteristic?

An immutable characteristic is (generally speaking) a characteristic about a person that they cannot change or should not be expected to change in a society that upholds individual freedom. These include, but are not limited to:

  • sex or gender;
  • race, skin colour, or ethnicity;
  • religious belief, including irreligion (such as atheism);
  • sexual or romantic orientation;
  • nationality, country of origin, citizenship status, or statelessness;
  • having a disability or chronic disease;

Under the law of the Australian Capital Territory, "serious vilification" on a number of grounds in the context of a "threatening act" is a serious criminal offence and such acts may be reported to authorities.

As rule of thumb, if you are attacking a person and not their ideas, you are likely using hate speech.
